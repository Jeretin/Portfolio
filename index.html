Red Milk

Genre: Horror

Platform: PC

Game Engine: UE5

Published: 12/2024

Download: itch.io

Red Milk is a horror game made by Unreal Engine where you wake up in the barn and have to get out there alive

Red Milk was my second game made by Unreal Engine. In the project I wanted to learn more about AI and that's why I focused on making the AI for the game. Game has two AI characters: one that chases you and one friend that you need to save. I learned a lot from the project and while I'm not totally happy with the enemy AI I made, I do know now what I would do next better. For example, I don't think the game needed as complex AI character as I tried to make. AI doesn't have to work perfectly when player doesn't see it. Sometimes it's okay to make player think that the AI works more complex than it actually does.

I also made some smaller things like part of movement system, one number combination lock minigame and I put some of the sounds in game. AI sounds and background voices. There is videos of those seen by clicking the picture on the left.

Platform: VR

Game Engine: UE5

Published: 5/2024

Brews & Brawls is VR bartendering game in fantasy world. You can serve drinks for goblins, fight with goblins or throw and break objects

Brews & Brawls was my first game project made for VR. It was a new experience for me and it teached me a lot. I wasn't very familiar with Unreal Engine either. I had done one very small project with it year before but almost everything was forgotten already. Most of the project was learning how to use Unreal Engine and how to make things work in VR. I didn't make much for the project but I learned a lot about working with the engine. I studied Unreal Engine's destruction system and how to break objects in VR with it.

Ranger's Reserve

Genre: City builder

Platform: Android

Game Engine: Unity

Published: 12/2023

Download: itch.io

Ranger's Reserve is a city builder game developed for android. In the game you can buy habitat's where you put animals living in. By leveling up you can access more animals and habitats. You can also feed the animals and level them up.

The game was my first and currently only game developed for mobile devices. Also making a city builder game was a new experience for me. Along with those I learned a lot new stuff about programming on C# and Unity. I learned how to use dictionaries and lists to make the game more efficient and to make different animals and habitats more logically. Also, all the names of animals are made by me. I'm the one you can blame for those.
